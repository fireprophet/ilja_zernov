Who I Am

I was born in Togliatti in a large family. From childhood, I was interested in socio-political issues, became a civil activist and volunteer at the human rights organization OVD-Info. Later, I enrolled at Kazan Federal University to study International Relations.

Since February 24, 2022, I have actively spoken out against the war and organized protest actions. Part of my family is from Ukraine, from Dnipro and Dnipropetrovsk region, so for me, the question of Ukraine's sovereignty and territorial integrity was obvious. Besides the internal disgust and moral rejection of the war, this became a personal topic for me.

Due to persecution by the state, I had to leave for Serbia. There, I initially worked illegally in a factory under difficult conditions, and later engaged in journalism and activist work. Eventually, I managed to move to Germany.

Currently, I work in a kindergarten as part of the Federal Voluntary Service (BFD). I have been living in Germany for two years and am intensively learning German. Since arriving in Leipzig, I have grown attached to this city, love it, and want to stay here. In the future, I plan to enroll at university.

Emigration has changed me - and for the better. I have learned to listen to people, find compromises, be more tolerant and bolder in defending rights. My path to Germany is a story about how others helped me: with housing, documents, advice. Now I want to do good things for others so that no one is left without support.

Like all migrants, I face bureaucratic difficulties, anxieties about the future, and everyday challenges. However, I understand that many people face even greater obstacles. Working in the Integration and Foreigners Council is an opportunity to support people, defend their rights and interests. This is exactly what I want to do.
